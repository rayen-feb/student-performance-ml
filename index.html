Key Findings

Data-driven insights from analyzing 1,044 student records across multiple dimensions.

  • 1
    Attendance is the strongest predictor of student performance, showing consistent correlation across all grade levels.
  • 2
    Study time shows significant positive correlation with final grades, with diminishing returns beyond 10 hours weekly.
  • 3
    Past failures are strong negative indicators, with each previous failure reducing predicted grades by approximately 2 points.
  • 4
    Parental education, especially mother's education level, positively influences student outcomes with a 0.22 correlation.
  • 5
    Alcohol consumption negatively impacts grades, with workday drinking showing stronger correlation than weekend consumption.
